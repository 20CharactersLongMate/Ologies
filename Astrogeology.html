Astrogeology

  • The geology and geochemistry of the planets and moons in the solar system.
  • The role of impact events (such as meteorite impacts and collisions) in shaping the geology of celestial bodies.
  • The role of volcanism and tectonics in the formation and evolution of celestial bodies.
  • The study of the geology and geochemistry of asteroids, comets, and meteorites.
  • The use of remote sensing and imaging techniques to study the geology of celestial bodies.
  • The use of geochemical and isotopic techniques to understand the history and evolution of celestial bodies.
  • The study of the geology and geochemistry of the outer solar system, including the Kuiper Belt and the Oort Cloud.
  • The role of water, ice, and other volatiles in the geology of celestial bodies.
  • The search for evidence of past or present life on other planets and moons.
  • The use of geologic data to understand the history and evolution of the solar system.
  • The study of the geology and geochemistry of exoplanets (planets orbiting other stars).
  • The role of impact craters in the geology of celestial bodies.
  • The study of the geology and geochemistry of the Moon.
  • The use of geologic data to understand the formation and evolution of planetary systems around other stars.
  • The study of the geology and geochemistry of Mars.
  • The use of geologic data to understand the history and evolution of the Earth.
