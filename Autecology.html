Autecology

  • The ecological factors that influence the distribution and abundance of a species.
  • The impact of a species on its environment and the role it plays in ecosystem dynamics.
  • The behavior of a species and its adaptations to its environment.
  • The population biology of a species, including factors that influence population growth and decline.
  • The role of a species in nutrient cycling and energy flow in an ecosystem.
  • The impacts of climate change on a species and its habitat.
  • The impacts of human activity on a species and its habitat.
  • The impacts of disease on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of predation on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of competition for resources on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of parasitism on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of mutualistic relationships (symbiotic relationships that benefit both species involved) on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of commensalism (symbiotic relationships in which one species benefits while the other is neither benefited nor harmed) on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of habitat destruction and degradation on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of invasions by non-native species on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of genetic diversity and evolution on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of environmental toxins and pollutants on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of conservation efforts on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The impacts of restoration efforts on a species and its population dynamics.
  • The use of autecological data to inform conservation and management efforts.
